# Watchguard MDR

CIS Controls v8 safeguard mappings for **Watchguard MDR**.

| CIS Safeguard | Safeguard Title | Alignment | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17.1 | Designate Personnel to Manage Incident Handling | Validates; Full | WatchGuard MDR designates dedicated analysts to manage incidents as they occur, backed by a full operations team to ensure continuity. Customers maintain oversight while the MDR team coordinates investigation, triage, and documentation. This hybrid approach ensures accountability while removing the need to staff internal resources around the clock. Oversight responsibilities remain with at least one customer contact, consistent with CIS expectations. |
| 17.2 | Establish and Maintain Contact Information for Reporting Security Incidents | Validates; Full | The WatchGuard MDR portal maintains up-to-date contact details for escalation and reporting. Customers always have a defined path to communicate with analysts and can escalate to other parties if needed. The MDR team also maintains contact readiness for external parties (e.g., insurance providers, law enforcement) where required. Contact information is reviewed regularly to ensure accuracy. |
| 17.3 | Establish and Maintain an Enterprise Process for Reporting Incidents | Validates; Full | The WatchGuard Defense portal and WatchGuard MDR workflow provide a clear process for reporting and tracking incidents. Cases are logged with required details such as type, severity, timeframe, and point of contact. The process is standardized and available to all customer personnel with reporting responsibilities. This ensures consistent handling and documentation across the enterprise. |
| 17.4 | Establish and Maintain an Incident Response Process | Validates; Full | WatchGuard MDR embeds an incident response process aligned with ISO frameworks, covering identification, containment, eradication, recovery, and review. Roles and responsibilities are documented and communicated at the start of each engagement. Customers can integrate their compliance requirements and communication plans directly into the WatchGuard MDR workflow. This creates a unified, repeatable process across all incidents. |
| 17.5 | Assign Key Roles and Responsibilities | Validates; Full | Key roles such as incident analyst/handler known as the threat hunter, and customer liaison (Technical Account Manager) are pre-assigned within WatchGuard MDR. Customers are engaged where IT or communications staff need to be involved. The WatchGuard MDR team manages technical response while enabling business leaders to focus on risk and impact decisions. |
| 17.6 | Define Mechanisms for Communicating During Incident Response | Validates; Full | WatchGuard MDR establishes secure, primary channels for communication such as portal messaging, secure email/cases, and phone escalation. Notifications are automatically generated as cases progress through different stages. Alternate channels are maintained in case of disruption (e.g., when email is unavailable). This layered approach ensures communication continuity during an incident. |
| 17.7 | Conduct Routine Incident Response Exercises | Validates | WatchGuard MDR can facilitate incident response exercises, including tabletop and live simulations, in coordination with customer staff. These exercises test communication paths, escalation procedures, and decision-making workflows. Findings are documented and may be incorporated into updated playbooks. At minimum, these exercises should be conducted annually to validate readiness. |
| 17.8 | Conduct Post-Incident Reviews | Validates; Full | While the internal reference to an "Incident" at WatchGuard does not have the same meaning we refer to the CIS context here as a "Breach" internally and in documentation. Every breach is followed by a review to document the timeline, root cause, and resolution steps. The WatchGuard MDR team prepares a structured report with lessons learned and recommendations for strengthening defenses. This ensures continuous improvement in the incident response program. |
| 17.9 | Establish and Maintain Security Incident Thresholds | Validates; Full | Thresholds for classifying security events and incidents are jointly defined by the WatchGuard MDR team and the customer. WatchGuard MDR analysts tune detection logic to differentiate between normal activity, suspicious behavior, and true incidents. These thresholds are regularly refined to reduce false positives while ensuring real threats are escalated quickly. Annual reviews ensure alignment with enterprise risk tolerance and regulatory requirements. |
